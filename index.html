ILLINOIS SEAL OF BILITERACY

Unlock college credit, career opportunities, and global connections.

What is it?

The Seal of Biliteracy is an award given by the State of Illinois to high school graduates who have demonstrated a high level of proficiency in English and one or more additional languages. It appears on your diploma and transcript as an official credential.

College Credit

Earn up to 12 credit hours at IL public universities.

Career Boost

Stand out to employers with verified language skills.

Global Citizen

Connect with diverse cultures and communities.

1

Save Time & Money

By law, all Illinois public universities and community colleges must accept the Seal of Biliteracy as equivalent to 2 years of foreign language coursework.

  • Skip Intro Classes: Jump straight into advanced coursework or minors.
  • Earn 8-12 Credits: That's nearly a full semester of college finished before you start.
  • Free College Credit: The Seal test costs a fraction of a 3-credit college course.

Note: Students must request credit within 3 academic years after graduating high school.

How Do You Earn It?

Step 1

English Proficiency

  • SAT Score: 480+ (EBRW)
  • ACT Score: 21+ (Composite)
  • AP English Exam: 4 or 5
  • ACCESS for ELLs: 4.8 Overall
Step 2

World Language Proficiency

  • AP Language Exam: 4 or 5
  • AAPPL Assessment: Intermediate High (I-5)
  • STAMP 4S: Intermediate High
  • International Baccalaureate (IB): 5+
